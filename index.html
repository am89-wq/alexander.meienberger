Research Profile

Dr. Alexander Meienberger

Managing Director, Center for Governance and Culture in Europe (GCE-HSG) — University of St.Gallen

My research centres on Russian literature of the nineteenth, twentieth, and twenty-first centuries, with a further focus on medical humanities. I work at the intersection of East European studies, literary and cultural studies, and biomedical ethics — examining how culture, ideology, and identity are negotiated in the Russian-speaking world.

Projects

since 2023

Contemporary Russian-Language Literature: Illness, Stigma, and Care

This ongoing research project examines how contemporary Russian-language literature represents illness, vulnerability, and care, and how literary narratives challenge the social and political dimensions of stigma. Situated at the intersection of Medical Humanities, Literary Studies, and East European Studies, the project investigates how contemporary literature engages with broader issues of health, ethics, and human dignity in the Russian-speaking world.

Doctoral Dissertation: The Foundation "Russkij Mir" as an Ideological Network

A doctoral thesis in Organisation and Culture at the University of St.Gallen analysing the "Russkij Mir" Foundation as a vehicle for ideology and soft power.
